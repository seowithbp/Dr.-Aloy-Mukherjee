Understanding Adrenal Gland Surgery

Adrenal gland surgery is a procedure performed to remove one or both adrenal glands when they develop tumors, become overactive, or cause hormonal imbalances. The adrenal glands are small organs located above the kidneys and play a vital role in producing hormones that regulate metabolism, blood pressure, and stress response. When these glands malfunction, it can lead to serious health issues that require surgical intervention. Today, Adrenal Gland Surgery in Delhi is performed using advanced techniques for safe and effective outcomes.

With modern medical advancements, adrenal gland procedures are now commonly performed using minimally invasive methods such as laparoscopic or robotic surgery. These techniques ensure smaller incisions, reduced pain, and faster recovery compared to traditional open surgery. Patients opting for Adrenal Gland Removal in Delhi benefit from expert care, accurate diagnosis, and improved long-term health outcomes.

Common Causes & Risk Factors

1. Adrenal Tumors

Benign or malignant tumors in the adrenal gland can disrupt hormone production and require surgical removal.

2. Hormonal Imbalance Disorders

Conditions like Cushing’s syndrome or Conn’s syndrome result from excessive hormone production and may need surgery.

3. Genetic Conditions

Certain inherited disorders can increase the risk of adrenal gland abnormalities.

4. High Blood Pressure

Uncontrolled hypertension caused by adrenal dysfunction may indicate the need for surgical treatment.

Identifying the Symptoms

1. Unexplained Weight Changes

Sudden weight gain or loss due to hormonal imbalance can be a key sign.

2. High Blood Pressure

Persistent or difficult-to-control blood pressure may indicate adrenal issues.

3. Fatigue and Weakness

Hormonal imbalance can lead to constant tiredness and low energy levels.

4. Hormonal Symptoms

Excessive sweating, mood changes, or irregular metabolism may occur.

Diagnosis Process

1. Blood and Urine Tests

These tests help evaluate hormone levels and detect abnormalities in adrenal function.

2. Imaging Tests

CT scans or MRI are used to identify tumors or structural issues in the adrenal glands.

3. Hormonal Evaluation

Detailed assessment is done to determine the type and severity of the condition.

Our Advanced Treatments

1. Laparoscopic Adrenalectomy

This minimally invasive procedure involves removing the adrenal gland through small incisions, ensuring faster recovery and minimal pain.

2. Robotic Adrenal Surgery

Robotic-assisted surgery provides enhanced precision and control, especially for complex cases, resulting in better outcomes and fewer complications.
