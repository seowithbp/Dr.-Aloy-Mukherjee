Frequently Asked Questions

What is Laparoscopic Surgery?

Laparoscopic surgery, or minimally invasive surgery, utilizes a specialized camera and tiny instruments inserted through small (0.5 - 1 cm) incisions to perform operations, avoiding large traditional cuts.

What are the benefits over open surgery?

The benefits are profound: significantly less post-operative pain, microscopic scarring, lower risk of wound infections, and a dramatically faster return to work and daily activities.

Is laparoscopic surgery safe?

Yes, it is the worldwide gold standard for abdominal surgeries. In the hands of a highly experienced surgeon like Dr. Aloy, it is actually safer and involves less blood loss than open surgery.

How long will I be in the hospital?

Most laparoscopic procedures, including gallbladder, hernia, and appendix surgeries, are performed as day-care or overnight procedures, meaning you go home within 24 hours.

Will I have prominent scars?

No. The tiny keyhole incisions typically heal very cleanly. With proper care, they fade into faint, barely noticeable lines within a few months.

Are there limitations to physical activity post-surgery?

While you will be walking the very same day, strenuous exercise and heavy lifting are usually restricted for 3 to 6 weeks to ensure the internal muscle layers heal correctly.
